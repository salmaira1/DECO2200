Week 1: Background Research

In the first lecture of the semester, Hamish Henderson states “A good user interface design encourages an easy, natural and engaging interaction between a user and a system, and it allows users to carry out their required task”. With this in mind throughout the breakdown of the design brief, I posed the following questions in relation to the task at hand: - What autonomous technology exists in the market? - What gap in the market am I trying to fill? - How will my design improve or innovate existing technology? A close analysis in the literature reveals interesting research conducted. In his study of autonomous technology, Omohundro (2014) explores how these systems are likely to behave in anti-social and harmful ways unless they are very carefully designed. In his article Experiencing Autonomous Vehicles: Crossing the Boundaries between a Drive and a Ride, Meschtscherjakov (2015) discusses the challenges and potentials for UX in autonomous vehicles. In summation, secondary research has revealed the need of efficient, careful design regarding autonomous technology. The user’s unfamiliarity may cause limitations that designers must keep in mind.
